Phase 01
Research & Discovery
🎯 Goal of This Phase

Understand what problems real users face in existing food delivery apps. You can't design solutions without knowing the problems first.

Step-by-Step: What to Do
  • 1
    Use 3 real apps for 2 days: Swiggy, Zomato, or Uber Eats. Order or browse normally. Write down every moment of confusion or frustration.
  • 2
    Competitor Analysis: Open each app side by side. Compare: home screen layout, search experience, restaurant page, checkout, and order tracking. Note what's good and what's bad.
  • 3
    Do 5-Question User Interviews: Ask 5–8 friends. Questions: What frustrates you most? Have you ever abandoned an order? What do you wish the app did differently?
  • 4
    List the Top 3 Problems: From your research, pick the 3 biggest pain points. Example: "Checkout has too many steps", "Can't filter by dietary preferences easily", "No clear delivery time estimate."
  • 5
    Write your Problem Statement: "Users aged 18–28 struggle to [problem] when [context] because [root cause]."
📋 Common Pain Points Found in Food Apps
Too many checkout steps Hard to filter food types No accurate ETA Confusing promo codes Poor search results No allergy info visible Reorder takes too long

Pick 1–2 of these to focus your redesign on. Don't try to fix everything.

Phase 02
User Persona
🎯 What is a User Persona?

A persona is a fictional character representing your typical user. It keeps you from designing for yourself — you design for them.

✏️ Create This Persona in Figma (FigJam or Frame)
  • 1
    Name & Photo: Give them a real-feeling name. Use a face illustration from UI Faces or avataaars.io (free).
  • 2
    Bio: Age 22. College student. Orders food 3x a week. Uses Swiggy mostly. Lives in a hostel.
  • 3
    Goals: Get food fast. Spend under ₹150. Find something healthy sometimes.
  • 4
    Frustrations: Too many steps to checkout. App crashes during payment. Can't find vegetarian options quickly.
  • 5
    Tech Habits: Uses phone 6h/day. Comfortable with UPI. Shops online frequently.
  • 6
    Quote: Write 1 sentence they might say. "I just want to order food in under 2 minutes."
🖥️
Figma Tip: Use FigJam for Persona Open FigJam → Use sticky notes for each section. Use coloured sections (pink = goals, red = frustrations, blue = habits). Screenshots look great in case studies.
Phase 03
Wireframing (Lo-Fi)
🎯 What is a Wireframe?

Grey-box sketches of your screens. No colours, no images. Just layout and structure. Think of it as the blueprint before the building.

Always wireframe BEFORE designing. It saves hours of wasted work.

📱 5 Screens You Must Design
  • 1
    Splash Screen — Logo + App name. Shown for 2 seconds when app opens.
  • 2
    Home Screen — Location, search bar, categories (Burger, Pizza, etc.), promotions banner, restaurant list.
  • 3
    Restaurant Page — Restaurant hero image, name + ratings + delivery time, menu items with photos and "Add" buttons.
  • 4
    Cart / Checkout — Item list, quantities, subtotal, delivery fee, total, "Place Order" button.
  • 5
    Order Tracking — Map with rider location, progress steps (Confirmed → Preparing → On the way → Delivered), ETA.
🖥️ How to Wireframe in Figma
  • 1
    New Figma file → Press F to open Frame tool → Choose iPhone 14 (390 × 844px)
  • 2
    Use only rectangles (R), text (T), and lines (L). Fill everything with grey (#CCCCCC for images, #EEEEEE for cards).
  • 3
    Label each element with a text box. E.g. "Search Bar", "Restaurant Card", "Category Icon"
  • 4
    Create all 5 frames side by side on the same canvas.
  • 5
    Show wireframes to 2 people. Ask: "What do you think you can do on each screen?" Note confusion.

🖥️ How to Build Each Screen in Figma
  • 1
    Start with your wireframe frame. Keep it underneath as reference. Duplicate it and start adding real colours on top.
  • 2
    Home Screen first. Add a real food image for the banner (use Unsplash.com — free). Replace grey boxes with your components.
  • 3
    Use Auto Layout everywhere. Select multiple items → Right click → Add Auto Layout (Shift+A). This keeps spacing consistent automatically.
  • 4
    Import free icons from the Figma Community: search "Heroicons" or "Phosphor Icons". Drag them in directly.
  • 5
    Add food images from Unsplash Plugin in Figma. Install it → select a rectangle → fill with photo instantly.
  • 6
    Add shadows. Select a card → Right panel → "+" under Drop shadow → Offset Y: 4, Blur: 12, Opacity: 10%.
  • 7
    Status bar & home indicator. Download a free iOS/Android status bar from Figma Community and paste at the top of each screen.
✓ DO
  • Use consistent 16px padding on all sides
  • Make touch targets at least 44×44px
  • Use real food images, not placeholders
  • Show ratings, delivery time, and price on every restaurant card
  • Add a bottom nav bar on all screens
✕ DON'T
  • Mix different font sizes randomly
  • Use more than 2–3 colours
  • Make buttons too small to tap
  • Forget the back button on inner screens
  • Use low quality or unrelated images
Phase 06
Prototyping & Testing
🎯 What is a Prototype?

Linking your screens so they feel like a real app you can tap through. No code needed — Figma does this automatically.

How to Prototype in Figma
  • 1
    Click the Prototype tab in the right panel (next to Design and Inspect).
  • 2
    Click a button (e.g. a restaurant card on the Home screen). Drag the blue arrow to your Restaurant screen.
  • 3
    Set the interaction: On Click → Navigate to → [Restaurant Screen]. Animation: Smart Animate, 300ms ease.
  • 4
    Connect all 5 screens in order: Splash → Home → Restaurant → Cart → Tracking.
  • 5
    Press ▶ Play button (top right). Your app is now clickable!
  • 6
    Share the prototype link with 3 people. Watch them use it silently. Note where they get confused.
🧪
Usability Testing Script (What to Say) "I'm going to share a food app prototype with you. Please think out loud as you try to order a Margherita Pizza. I'm testing the app, not you — there are no wrong answers." Then stay silent and observe.
📊 What to Document After Testing
  • →
    Where did users hesitate or get confused?
  • →
    Did they find the checkout button easily?
  • →
    What did they expect to happen that didn't?
  • →
    What did they say they liked?

Make 1–2 improvements based on feedback. Mention this iteration in your case study — it proves a real UX process.

Phase 07
Write the Case Study
🎯 Why It Matters

Your case study is what gets you hired. Recruiters spend 30 seconds on a resume and 5 minutes on a case study. It should tell a story: Problem → Process → Solution.

📝 Case Study Structure (for Behance or Notion)
  • 1
    Cover Image: Best-looking screen of your app, full width. First impression matters.
  • 2
    Project Overview: 2–3 sentences. What is the app, what problem does it solve, who is it for?
  • 3
    The Problem: Quote a real user frustration. Show competitor screenshots with red annotations.
  • 4
    Research: Share your interview findings. Use a simple chart or quote cards.
  • 5
    User Persona: Screenshot of your persona from FigJam.
  • 6
    Wireframes: Show your lo-fi wireframes. Explain key layout decisions.
  • 7
    Design System: Show your colour palette, type scale, and key components.
  • 8
    Final Screens: Full-size, beautiful screenshots. Use a phone mockup frame (free on Mockuphone.com).
  • 9
    Testing & Iterations: Before/after showing what you changed after user testing.
  • 10
    Learnings: 2–3 things you learned. Shows humility and growth mindset.
🔗
Always Include a Prototype Link At the top of your case study, add: "View Prototype →" linking to your Figma prototype. Recruiters want to click through the app. Make it the most prominent element.
✅ Final Project Checklist
  • 5 hi-fi screens completed in Figma
  • Clickable prototype linking all screens
  • Design system (colours + fonts + components)
  • User persona created in FigJam
  • Tested with at least 3 real users
  • Before/after improvement documented
  • Case study published on Behance or Notion
  • Prototype link in case study header
  • App mockup screenshots added to resume
